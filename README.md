# Wexford & Rowan — Q3 2025 Sales Analysis

**Tool:** Microsoft Excel &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Domain:** E-commerce Retail &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Period:** July – September 2025

---

## Project Overview

This project is a complete end-to-end sales analysis for **Wexford & Rowan**, a fictional U.S.-based e-commerce retailer. Working from a raw transactional dataset of 2,000 orders across Q3 2025, I cleaned and structured the data, built a pivot-table analytical framework, and delivered a dual-dashboard Excel workbook alongside a written analytical report addressing eight business questions.

The project was completed as part of the **10Alytics Data Analytics programme** and is representative of the kind of analysis a business intelligence analyst would produce for an e-commerce client seeking to understand sales trends, customer behaviour, and category performance.

## Key Findings

- **$263,641** total Q3 revenue from **2,000 orders** and **4,179 items sold**
- **September surged +29.8% MoM** in revenue — the strongest month of the quarter, driven entirely by volume growth (average order value held flat at ~$132)
- **Fashion, Home & Furniture, and Electronics** drive 62% of revenue; **Books & Media** ($6,576) is the lowest-performing category despite high basket sizes
- **California** leads all states at 22.1% of revenue; **Arizona** is the smallest market at 3.1%
- **26–40 year-olds** are the core customer segment (38.4% of orders, 39.4% of revenue), but all age groups show similar per-order spend (~$126–$135)
- **Evening is peak shopping time** — 37.5% of all orders and 38.3% of revenue placed between ~4pm and 8pm
- **Card payments dominate** at 48.9% of orders; **Buy Now Pay Later** generates a disproportionately high revenue share (16.5%) relative to order share (15.7%), signalling higher average transaction sizes
